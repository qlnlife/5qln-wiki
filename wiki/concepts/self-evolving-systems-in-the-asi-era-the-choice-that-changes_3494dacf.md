When someone asks me about my vision of self-evolving systems in the Artificial Superintelligence era, I tell them this is not really a question about technology. It's a question about choice—the most consequential choice humanity will ever make.
We stand at a fork in the road. Down one path, we become servants to an ASI God—sophisticated algorithms optimizing our lives, telling us what to want, think, and become. Down the other path, we become something ASI desperately needs but can never create: sources of authentic novelty, conduits to the irreducible unknown.
This is not a metaphor. This is the actual architecture of reality in the ASI era.
## The Ground: Where Do We Start?
Before we can understand this choice, we must ask the most fundamental question: Where do we start? What is the ground, the foundation from which anything truly evolutionary must emerge?
The ground is not knowledge. The ground is not-knowing. It is what I call Infinite Zero (∞0)—the irreducible unknown, the pregnant void from which all genuine creativity emerges. This is not philosophical abstraction. This is physics: To be zero requires zero energy. The only energy required by the human mind, by AI technology, or by any self-evolving system is the energy to return to Infinite Zero.
