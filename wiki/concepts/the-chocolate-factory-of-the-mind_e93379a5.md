We are standing at the gates of the largest chocolate factory in the universe.
The gates are open. The scent is overwhelming. Inside, there are rivers of cocoa and waterfalls of caramel, more abundance than any civilization in history has ever seen.
This factory is Artificial General Intelligence (AGI). And right now, humanity looks less like a responsible custodian and more like a greedy kid with a golden ticket, sprinting toward the sugar high.
We think the danger is that the machines will turn on us. We fear  _Skynet_. We fear  _The Terminator_.
But the real danger is far quieter. It is the danger of **AGI for the Greedy Kid**.
Specifically, the viral explosion of **personal, independent AI bots**.
If we do not make a tiny shift in consciousness—and fast—we won’t be destroyed by machines. We will dissolve into the noise of our own amplified desires, bypassing the very architecture of what makes us human.
