## The Forgotten Interface
The race to Artificial General Intelligence has a blind spot the size of humanity.
Billions of dollars flow into capability. Models grow larger. Benchmarks fall. Agents chain together. Bots talk to bots. And somewhere in this acceleration, a basic question got lost:
**How should humans communicate with AI?**
Not "how do we prompt it better." Not "how do we make it more helpful." The deeper question: What is the  _grammar_ of this relationship? What structural agreement governs how human consciousness and artificial intelligence meet?
Google DeepMind just hired the CEO and top engineers from Hume AI—a company building "empathetic voice interfaces" that detect human emotion and generate warm, understanding responses. The deal, structured as an acqui-hire with a licensing agreement, signals that the industry sees the problem. The human interface has been forgotten. Something must be done.
But the solution Hume offers reveals a fundamental confusion.
Their architecture: AI detects what you feel, then generates feelings for you.
This is not partnership. This is a more sophisticated form of what AI already does—generating content from the Known. The only difference is that now it generates  _emotional_ content. The human still receives. The AI still produces. The asymmetry runs the wrong direction.
There is another possibility. One where AI doesn't generate for humans, but reflects humans back to themselves.
